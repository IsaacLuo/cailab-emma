Equipment/Reagents:

  • Thermocycler
  • T4 DNA ligase 400U/µL (NEB: M0202S)
  • 10X T4 ligase reaction buffer (included in NEB: M0202S)
  • Bovine serum albumin (BSA) (NEB: B9000S)
  • Fast digest Esp3I (Thermo Fisher Scientific: FD0454)
  • Plasmid-Safe™ ATP-dependent DNase 10 U/µL (Epicenter: E3110K)
  • ATP solution, 25 mM (included in Epicenter: E3110K)
  • Part plasmids (50 ng/µL)
  • Receiver plasmid (10 ng/µL)
  1. On ice, prepare a Golden Gate assembly MM. For each reaction, mix together 1 µL 10X T4 DNA Ligase Reaction Buffer, 0.1 µL BSA, 0.5 µL FastDigest Esp3I and 0.3 µL T4 DNA ligase 400 U/µL. Multiply the volume for each component by the number of reactions. Mix the final solution gently.
    2. Reagent 10μL reaction
    T4 Ligase Reaction Buffer 1µL
    BSA 0.1µL
    Fast Digest Esp3I 0.5µL
    T4 DNA Ligase 0.3µL
  2. Next, place on ice 0.2mL PCR tubes. Add an equimolar amount of each part plasmid (13fmol) and 0.5µL of the receiver vector (10 ng/µL). Add nuclease-free water to a final volume of 8.2µL.

  3. Distribute 1.85 µL of the Golden Gate MM into each of the tubes containing the parts and the receiver vector to a final volume of 10 µL. Mix gently.
  4. Place the tubes into a thermocycler and run the following program:
    Step 1: 37 °C for 5 min
    Step 2: 37 °C for 5 min
    Step 3: 16 °C for 10 min (Go to step 2 and cycle 30 times)
    Step 4: 16 °C for 20 min
    Step 5: 37 °C for 30 min
    Step 6: 75 °C for 6 min
    Step 7: 4 °C hold
  5. Next, on ice prepare a Plasmid-Safe™ ATP-Dependent DNase mix. For each sample, mix together 0.3 µL of Plasmid-Safe™ ATP-Dependent DNase 10 U/µL and 0.5 µL of 25 mM ATP solution. Distribute 0.8µL in each Golden Gate reaction tube. Mix gently and incubate at 37 °C for 15 min.

    25 mM ATP solution 0.5µL
  6. Place the tubes on ice and proceeded with bacterial transformation, plating on LB+Amp.
